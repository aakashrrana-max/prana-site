How much does All-on-4 cost in Miami?

By Dr. Aakash Rana, DDS · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

Short answer: At Prana Dental in Edgewater, All-on-4 starts at $9,999 per arch — about $167/month with 0% financing (on approved credit). Across Miami, you'll often see $20,000–$30,000 per arch, mostly because the lab work is outsourced.

If you're researching All-on-4 (a full arch of fixed teeth on four implants), the first thing you'll notice is how much prices vary. Here's an honest breakdown of what drives the number, and how to compare quotes so you don't overpay — or underpay and regret it.

What goes into the price

An All-on-4 price should cover the surgery, the implants, and both the same-day provisional teeth and the final restoration. The biggest cost driver most patients never hear about is the dental lab. Most practices send your case to an outside lab and pass along that markup. Because our lab is in-house, we design and mill your arch on-site — faster, better-fitting, and without the middleman cost.

Typical Miami price ranges

  • Prana Dental: from $9,999 per arch (in-house lab, Dr. Rana places every case).
  • Corporate chains: often $20,000–$26,000 per arch, sometimes with add-ons quoted later.
  • Ultra-budget clinics: a low sticker price that can grow once extractions, bone grafting or the final teeth are added.

Financing: what "$167/month" really means

With 0% interest on approved credit, a $9,999 arch spread over 60 months is roughly $167/month. You can apply before your visit and most decisions come back in minutes — applying doesn't affect your credit score. Try different terms on our payment estimator to see what fits your budget.

How to compare quotes fairly

Ask three questions of any practice: Who actually performs the surgery? Is the lab in-house or outsourced? And is the price in writing with nothing added later? At Prana, the answers are: Dr. Rana personally, in-house, and yes. Learn more on our All-on-4 page or see full transparent pricing.

The bottom line

All-on-4 is a life-changing procedure, and the cheapest quote isn't always the best value — but neither is the most expensive. The smartest move is a free consult with a 3D scan so you get a real, written number for your mouth, not a range from the internet.
