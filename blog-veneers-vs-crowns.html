Veneers vs. crowns: which is right for you?

By Dr. Aakash Rana, DDS · Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

Short answer: Choose a veneer to beautify a healthy tooth, and a crown to rebuild and protect a damaged one. At Prana, both porcelain options start at $999 — so the decision is about your tooth, not the price tag.

Patients often ask for "veneers" when what they actually need is a crown, or the other way around. Both can give you a gorgeous, natural-looking result — they just solve different problems. Here's how to tell which one fits your smile.

What a veneer does

A porcelain veneer is an ultra-thin shell bonded to the front of a tooth. It's a cosmetic upgrade: it changes color, shape, size and alignment while keeping most of your natural tooth. Veneers shine for healthy front teeth that you simply want to look brighter, straighter or more even.

What a crown does

A crown covers the entire tooth like a helmet. It's the right call when a tooth is cracked, heavily filled, worn down, or has had a root canal, because it restores full strength as well as appearance. If there isn't enough healthy structure to bond a veneer to, a crown is the durable choice.

Quick comparison

  • Best for cosmetics on healthy teeth: veneer.
  • Best for damaged, cracked or root-canaled teeth: crown.
  • Tooth reduction: minimal for veneers, more for crowns.
  • Starting price at Prana: $999 either way.

How we help you decide

Because we run an in-house lab, we design either option to your face and let you preview the result digitally before anything is made — so you approve the look first. Many "smile makeovers" are actually a mix: veneers on the healthy teeth, crowns where teeth need rebuilding. The goal is always the most conservative option that gives you a result that lasts.

The bottom line

Don't pick between veneers and crowns from an article — pick from an exam. A free consult includes a 3D scan and a digital smile design, so you'll see exactly what each option would look like on your teeth before you commit.
