What a crown can fix

Strength and shape, restored.

A crown caps a damaged tooth to protect what's left and bring back full, comfortable function. Here are the most common reasons our patients need one.

◇

Cracked or broken tooth

A crown holds a fractured tooth together and shields it from further damage, so you can bite and chew with confidence again.

◇

Large old filling

When a filling is too big to last, a crown takes over and protects the remaining tooth structure for the long run.

◇

After a root canal

A treated tooth becomes brittle. A crown caps and reinforces it, protecting your saved natural tooth.

◇

Worn-down teeth

Grinding and years of wear flatten teeth. A crown rebuilds the height and shape so your bite feels right again.

◇

Cosmetic reshaping

A misshapen or discolored tooth can be reshaped and brightened with a crown that's matched to your smile.

◇

Protecting a weak tooth

For a tooth weakened by decay or stress, a crown adds a strong, protective cap before it breaks.

Questions

Crowns, answered.

How much does a dental crown cost in Miami?

Porcelain and zirconia crowns start at $999 per crown at Prana. Because every tooth is different, your exact plan is confirmed in writing at your free consult. We accept most major dental insurance, and flexible 0% financing is available for anything not covered.

How long does a crown last?

With good care, a well-made porcelain or zirconia crown commonly lasts 10 to 15 years or more. Because ours are designed and crafted in-house for a precise fit, they're built to protect your tooth for the long run.

Does getting a crown hurt?

Most patients are surprised how comfortable it is. We work gently under local anesthetic, oral sedation is available if you'd like it, and we move at your pace.

What's the difference between a crown and a veneer?

A veneer is a thin shell bonded to the front of a tooth for cosmetic reasons. A crown caps the entire tooth to restore and protect it after damage, decay or a root canal. Dr. Rana will recommend whichever is right for you.

Can I get a crown in one day?

Because our lab is in-house, many cases finish in just a couple of visits, far faster than practices that outsource to an outside lab. We'll confirm your exact timeline at your consult.

Do you accept insurance for crowns?

Yes. We accept most major dental insurances and help you get the most from your benefits. For anything not covered, 0% financing is available on approved credit.
